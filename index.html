Linearity Lesson Page

Linearity is one of the simplest ideas in mathematics, yet it opens the door to some of the most powerful tools we use in science, engineering, data analysis, and beyond. At its core, linearity is about relationships that behave predictably: when you double the input, the effect doubles with it; when you combine inputs, their effects combine just as neatly. That simple structure is what makes linear algebra such a reliable language for understanding complex systems.
